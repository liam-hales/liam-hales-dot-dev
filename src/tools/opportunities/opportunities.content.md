# Opportunities

Liam's stance on new roles — what he values, what would tempt him to move,
and how he prefers to be approached.

## Current Status

- Not actively looking, but curious and open to the right opportunity
- A passive candidate — content where he is, so anything new has to be worth the move


## What He Values in a Role

- Strong team and engineering culture
- Interesting technical problems to work on
- Room for growth and continuous learning
- Autonomy and ownership over his work
- A healthy work-life balance
- Fully remote working


## Direction & Focus

- Looking for a senior (or similar) position that stays hands-on with the code/engineering
- Wants to work on a company's own core technology and product, not a consultancy building for external clients
- Open across frontend, backend, and full-stack work
- Keen to keep working within his core ecosystem

> Full technical capability and stack detail is described in the `skills` tool.


## What Would Him Move

- A strong engineering culture with real standards, not standards on paper
- Modern tooling and tech, with an appetite to keep improving it
- Trust and low micromanagement — being given the room to do the job well


## Dealbreakers

- Heavy micromanagement
- Poor code quality or an absence of standards
- Legacy tech with no appetite to modernise
- An always-on culture or poor work-life balance
- In-office mandates — the role must be fully remote


## Office Visits

- The office needs to be within a reasonable travel distance
- Parking has to be sorted — if it's a nightmare to find or comes out of his own pocket, forget it
- Fully remote is the baseline for day-to-day work — happy to come in for things that genuinely warrant being in person
- Sprint ceremonies, planning days, workshops, social events — all good reasons to travel in
- Won't come in for meetings that could just as easily be a Teams call

> Remote-only is about how the work gets done, not avoiding the team.
> In-person time has its place — it just has to earn it.


## Compensation

- Values transparency on salary from the outset — expects a real figure or range up front, not vague language
- Has no time for the term "competitive salary" — it signals evasiveness rather than openness
- Not money-driven — however salary is a massive factor and being upfront about it is a sign of respect for his time


## How to Approach Him

- Must be contacted via LinkedIn only
- Given he's not actively looking, an approach needs to lead with what makes the opportunity genuinely exceptional
